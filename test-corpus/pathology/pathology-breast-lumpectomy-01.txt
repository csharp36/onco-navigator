GROSS DESCRIPTION
-----------------
Received fresh in the operating room, labeled "left breast lumpectomy," is an

is oriented with a short suture marking the superior margin and a long suture
marking the lateral margin. The cut surface reveals a firm, stellate, tan-white
mass measuring 1.8 x 1.5 x 1.2 cm, located 0.8 cm from the closest (anterior)

Representative sections submitted in cassettes A1-A12.

Also received, labeled "sentinel lymph node," are two lymph nodes measuring
1.2 cm and 0.8 cm in greatest dimension. Each bisected and submitted entirely
in cassettes B1-B2.

MICROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION
-----------------------
Sections of the breast mass demonstrate an infiltrating ductal carcinoma, not
otherwise specified (NOS), arranged in nests and cords with associated
desmoplastic stromal reaction. The tumor cells exhibit moderate nuclear
pleomorphism with occasional mitotic figures (8 mitoses per 10 high-power
fields). No lymphovascular invasion is identified. Ductal carcinoma in situ
(DCIS) of intermediate nuclear grade, solid and cribriform patterns, is present
within and adjacent to the invasive component, comprising approximately 15% of
the tumor volume.

Margins: The closest surgical margin is the anterior margin at 0.8 cm. All
other margins are greater than 1.0 cm. No tumor is present at the inked
margins.

Sentinel lymph nodes (0/2): Both lymph nodes are negative for metastatic
carcinoma by H&E and immunohistochemistry (AE1/AE3).
