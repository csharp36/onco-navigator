INDICATIONS
-----------
64-year-old male with right upper lobe adenocarcinoma diagnosed on CT-guided
core needle biopsy (2026-02-15). Tumor measures 4.8 cm on CT. PET-CT
demonstrated FDG-avid right upper lobe mass (SUV max 10.1) with a single
FDG-avid right hilar lymph node (station 10R, SUV max 4.2). No distant
metastases. Pulmonary function testing showed FEV1 of 2.4 L (82% predicted),
adequate for lobectomy. Multidisciplinary tumor board recommended surgical
resection.

DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE
-------------------------
The patient was brought to the operating room and placed in the left lateral
decubitus position. After satisfactory induction of general endotracheal
anesthesia with a left-sided double-lumen endotracheal tube, the right lung
was isolated and deflated. The right chest was prepped and draped in the usual
sterile fashion. A time-out was performed.

VATS ACCESS:
A 2 cm utility incision was made in the anterior 4th intercostal space. Two
additional 1 cm port incisions were placed in the 7th intercostal space at the
mid-axillary line and posterior axillary line. A 30-degree thoracoscope was
introduced.

EXPLORATION:
The pleural space was inspected. No pleural effusion or pleural implants. The
tumor was identified in the right upper lobe as a firm mass approximately 5 cm
in greatest dimension, not involving the chest wall or mediastinum. The fissures
were complete.

RIGHT UPPER LOBECTOMY:
The right upper lobe pulmonary vein was identified and dissected circumfer-
entially. The vein was divided using an endovascular stapler (Echelon Flex 45 mm
white load). The truncus anterior (apical and anterior segmental arteries) was
identified, dissected, and divided with an endovascular stapler (Echelon Flex
45 mm white load). The posterior ascending artery was identified and divided
with an endovascular stapler. The right upper lobe bronchus was identified and
dissected. A frozen section of the bronchial margin was submitted and returned
negative for malignancy. The bronchus was divided with an endobronchial stapler
(Echelon Flex 45 mm green load). The minor fissure was completed with multiple
firings of an endoscopic stapler (Echelon Flex 60 mm blue load). The specimen
was placed in a retrieval bag and removed through the utility incision.

MEDIASTINAL LYMPH NODE DISSECTION:
Systematic mediastinal lymph node dissection was performed. Lymph node stations
2R, 4R, 7, 8, and 9 were dissected and submitted as separate specimens.

CLOSURE:
The bronchial stump was tested under saline with no air leak. Hemostasis was
confirmed. A 28 Fr chest tube was placed through the posterior port site into
the pleural apex. The lung was re-expanded under direct vision. Port sites were
closed in layers with 2-0 Vicryl and 4-0 Monocryl subcuticular sutures.

Estimated blood loss: 200 mL.
Fluids administered: 2,200 mL crystalloid.
Complications: None.

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was extubated in the operating room.
Transferred to the thoracic surgical ICU for overnight monitoring.
