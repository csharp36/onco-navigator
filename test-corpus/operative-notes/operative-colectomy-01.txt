INDICATIONS
-----------
70-year-old male with ascending colon adenocarcinoma identified on colonoscopy
(2026-03-01) and confirmed on biopsy. CT abdomen/pelvis demonstrated a 4.5 cm
mass in the ascending colon with two suspicious pericolonic lymph nodes. CEA
preoperatively: 12.4 ng/mL (elevated). No evidence of distant metastatic
disease on CT chest/abdomen/pelvis or PET-CT. Multidisciplinary tumor board
recommended surgical resection followed by adjuvant chemotherapy given suspected
nodal involvement.

DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE
-------------------------
The patient was brought to the operating room and placed in the supine modified
lithotomy position with arms tucked. Sequential compression devices were applied
bilaterally. After satisfactory induction of general endotracheal anesthesia,
an epidural catheter was placed at T8-T9 for postoperative pain management.
The abdomen was prepped and draped in the usual sterile fashion. A time-out was
performed confirming patient identity, procedure, and surgical site.

PORT PLACEMENT:
Pneumoperitoneum was established via Veress needle at Palmer's point to 15 mmHg.
A 12 mm optical trocar was placed at the umbilicus for the camera. Additional
ports: 5 mm left lower quadrant, 5 mm suprapubic, and 12 mm left flank.

EXPLORATION:
Diagnostic laparoscopy revealed no peritoneal carcinomatosis, liver surface
metastases, or ascites. The ascending colon mass was identified as a bulky
lesion in the mid-ascending colon with serosal involvement. No intraperitoneal
adhesions.

MOBILIZATION:
The right colon was mobilized in a medial-to-lateral approach. The ileocolic
pedicle was identified and the ileocolic artery and vein were individually
isolated, clipped, and divided. The right branch of the middle colic artery was
identified and divided. Complete mobilization of the right colon including the
hepatic flexure was performed with identification and preservation of the right
ureter, duodenum (second portion), and right gonadal vessels. The greater
omentum was detached from the transverse colon along the planned resection line.

EXTRACTION AND ANASTOMOSIS:
A 5 cm midline periumbilical extraction incision was made. The mobilized right
colon was delivered through a wound protector. The ileum was divided 8 cm
proximal to the ileocecal valve using a GIA 80 mm blue load stapler. The
transverse colon was divided at the junction of the proximal and middle third
using a GIA 80 mm blue load stapler. The right hemicolectomy specimen was
removed and sent to pathology.

A functional end-to-end (side-to-side) ileocolic anastomosis was created using
a GIA 80 mm blue load stapler through aligned enterotomies. The common
enterotomy was closed with a TA 60 mm blue load stapler. The anastomosis was
inspected and noted to be patent with no tension, good blood supply, and intact
staple lines. The mesenteric defect was closed with 3-0 Vicryl running suture.

CLOSURE:
The bowel was returned to the abdominal cavity. The extraction site fascia was
closed with #1 PDS in a figure-of-eight fashion. Pneumoperitoneum was
re-established and final laparoscopic inspection confirmed hemostasis, intact
anastomosis, and no injuries. Ports were removed under direct vision. Port site
fascia (12 mm sites) closed with 0 Vicryl. Skin closed with 4-0 Monocryl
subcuticular sutures. Steri-Strips applied.

Estimated blood loss: 120 mL.
Fluids administered: 2,800 mL lactated Ringer's.
Urine output: 450 mL (adequate throughout case).
Complications: None.

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was transferred to the PACU in
stable condition. Epidural infusion initiated for postoperative pain management.
