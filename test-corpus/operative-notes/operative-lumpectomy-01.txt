DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE
-------------------------
The patient was brought to the operating room and placed in the supine position.
After satisfactory induction of general endotracheal anesthesia, the left chest
and axilla were prepped and draped in the usual sterile fashion. A time-out was
performed confirming patient identity, procedure, and surgical site.

SENTINEL LYMPH NODE BIOPSY:
Technetium-99m sulfur colloid was injected periareolarly by the nuclear medicine
team prior to the procedure. Using a handheld gamma probe, a hot spot was
identified in the left axilla. A 3 cm transverse incision was made in the
axillary skin crease. Dissection was carried through subcutaneous tissue to
the axillary fascia. The gamma probe guided identification of two sentinel
lymph nodes with counts of 1,840 and 620 (background 12). Both nodes were
excised and sent fresh for intraoperative touch prep cytology. Touch prep
was reported as NEGATIVE for metastatic carcinoma for both nodes.

LUMPECTOMY:
The left breast mass at the 2 o'clock position was localized by palpation. A
curvilinear incision was made overlying the mass. Dissection was carried through
subcutaneous tissue and breast parenchyma circumferentially around the mass with
a goal of achieving 1 cm gross margins. The specimen was oriented with a short
suture placed superiorly and a long suture placed laterally. The deep margin was
carried to the pectoralis fascia, which was excised en bloc with the specimen.

Specimen radiography confirmed the presence of the mass within the specimen with
adequate margins. The specimen was sent to pathology in formalin.

The lumpectomy cavity was irrigated with warm saline. Hemostasis was achieved
with electrocautery. Cavity margin clips (titanium) were placed at the
superior, inferior, medial, lateral, anterior, and deep aspects of the cavity
for future radiation planning. The breast tissue was re-approximated with
3-0 Vicryl interrupted sutures. Skin was closed with 4-0 Monocryl subcuticular
suture and Dermabond.

The axillary incision was closed in layers: axillary fascia with 3-0 Vicryl,
subcutaneous tissue with 3-0 Vicryl, and skin with 4-0 Monocryl subcuticular
suture and Dermabond.

Estimated blood loss: 30 mL.

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was transferred to the PACU in
stable condition.
